- pitch: meditative action rpg, isometric top-down, pixel art rendered as 2d billboards in a 3d world, dark fantasy
- design pillars:
    - meditative — player is not rushed; process matters more than result
    - grinding as enjoyment, not punishment
    - meaning through settlement — npcs and building are the goal, not background
    - no explicit classes — playstyle is defined by equipment
- gameloop: go into the world -> find loot, resources, npcs -> return to settlement -> build, house npcs, restock, upgrade gear -> push to harder locations
- world layout: split into locations connected by nodes; surface locations and underground dungeons; each location has varying ground heights (highground / lowground); danger scales with distance from start
- character build: six equipment slots define playstyle — weapon (combat stats, attack type), armor (defense), clothing (stat and niche bonuses), boots (movement), belt (consumable count and types), bag (inventory size)
- enemy categories: humanoids, beasts, monsters
- resources in world: wood, stone, hides, cotton, iron ore (more ore types planned), hay, several flower types, food (meat, wheat, pumpkin, corn), gold; loot also drops from chests, pouches, backpacks
- coop: planned 2-4 players; architectural decision must be locked before layer 2 of dev because it constrains saving, world state, and npc behavior
- aesthetic: pixel art, stylized geometry (not realistic), 2d characters in a 3d world, dark fantasy palette
- out of scope: archery as a starting weapon line
